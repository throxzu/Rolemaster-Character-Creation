## 6.0 Overview

Starting characters begin with appropriate clothing and buy additional equipment using starting money. All listed items are medium size.

**Size scaling**: multiply cost and weight by ×4 per size above medium (×4 Big, ×16 Large, ×64 Huge); divide by ÷4 per size below medium (÷4 Small, ÷16 Tiny, ÷64 Diminutive).

## 6.1 Breakage

**Trigger**: any maneuver resulting in a natural roll of **33 or 77** (even on an open-ended roll); or when equipment is used outside its design purpose; or on fumble/critical results in combat.

**Roll**: d100OE + item Strength + quality modifier − accumulated damage penalties. Consult Table 6-1.

**Armor breakage**: penalty applies as armor maneuver penalty. If total exceeds −50, that area is no longer protected.

**Ammunition**: single breakage roll per ammo type; use Ammo column for % recovered.

### Table 6-1: Equipment Breakage

| Roll | Result | Ammo Recovered |
|------|--------|----------------|
| < 1 | **Absolute Failure** — item is broken | 0% |
| 1 – 75 | **Failure** — item damaged; −20 to all uses until repaired | 10% |
| UM 66 | **Unusual Event** — if failure: damage is temporary; if success: prior damage reduced by up to 10 | 25% |
| 76 – 100 | **Partial Success** — item at −10 until repaired | 50% |
| 101 – 175 | **Success** — item holds; no penalty | 75% |
| 176+ | **Absolute Success** — item appears impervious; no damage | 100% |

**Modifiers**: +ST (item's inherent strength); +Qual (quality/material); −Dam (accumulated damage penalties from previous checks).

### Breaking Objects Deliberately

Blows hit automatically (no attack roll). Item makes immediate breakage check minus attacker's Strength bonus and any situational factors. Very strong objects (walls, boulders: strength 200+) require sustained effort:

| Tool Quality | Strength Reduction |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Optimal tool (e.g., pickaxe for stone) | −20/minute |
| Acceptable tool (e.g., hammer for stone) | −10/minute |
| Poor tool (e.g., sword for stone) | −1 to 2/minute; tool also checks breakage |

In tactical situations: breakage roll when object strength drops below 200, then once per round as strength continues to decrease.

## 6.2 Repairing Items

Requires appropriate crafting skill + materials. Absolute Maneuver; GM sets difficulty. Accumulated damage penalty applies to the repair roll. Broken outright → additional −100 to roll.

### Table 6-2: Equipment Repair

| Roll | Result |
|------|--------|
| < 1 | **Absolute Failure** — item breaks; if already broken, unrepairable |
| 1 – 75 | **Failure** — item remains broken/damaged |
| UM 66 | **Unusual Event** — jury-rigged: functional on Partial Success or higher, non-functional on Failure or lower |
| 76 – 100 | **Partial Success** — broken item cannot be fully repaired; damage penalties reduced by half |
| 101 – 175 | **Success** — damaged item fully repaired; broken item fixed with permanent −10 penalty |
| 176+ | **Absolute Success** — item restored as good as new |

**Repair skills**: Leather-working (leather items), Metal-working (metal armor/weapons, requires forge), Wood-working (wooden items/structures).

## Chapter 7: Weapons & Armor Rules

### Improvised Weapons

When using a mundane item as a weapon, use the closest existing attack table and apply a penalty (typically −15 to −50). Adjust critical type, attack size, fumble range, and range modifiers as appropriate. If the combatant lacks the relevant skill, apply the Similar Skills penalty (see skills.md) on top.

### 7.1 Weapon Categories

**All melee weapon skills use St/St/Ag for stat bonuses.**
**All ranged weapon skills use Ag/Ag/St for stat bonuses.**

Two-handed melee weapons (Greater Blade, Greater Chain, Greater Hafted, Pole Arm) are treated as **one size larger** and gain a **+10 bonus** on their attack.

#### Melee Weapon Specializations

| Category | Weapons |
|----------|---------|
| Blade | Broadsword, dagger, falchion, arming sword, rapier, scimitar, short sword |
| Greater Blade | Claymore, great falchion, long scimitar, longsword |
| Chain | Flail |
| Greater Chain | Heavy flail |
| Hafted | Battle axe, club, fighting stick, mace, war hammer |

**Pole Arm special rules**: Cover DB modifiers are halved — attacks from second rank incur +20 DB (vs. +40 for other melee). Pole arms can be braced against charges (Section 8.2).

**Shield Bash**: uses Shield skill as OB; when attacking, shield provides no DB except from OB placed in parry.

Ranged attacks **cannot be parried** and cannot be used to parry. Point-blank bonus (+20) applies at ≤10'. Vambraces impose ranged attack penalties (see Table 6-4).

### 7.2 Armor

**Armor Type (AT)** 1–10 is based on material and construction, assuming a full suit. ATs do not represent specific armor — they represent a family of armors with similar flexibility and material properties.

**Maneuver penalties** apply to movement and physical maneuvers but **not** Combat Training skills (OB and parry). Maneuver penalties (only) can be reduced with Battle Expertise: Maneuvering in Armor.

**Donning/removing**: 1 round per −5 maneuver penalty to don; half that to remove. Donning in half time incurs additional −20 penalty until properly adjusted.

**Natural armor**: no maneuver penalties; no RR bonus vs. spells; does not interfere with spellcasting.

#### Piecemeal Armor

When not wearing a full suit or wearing mismatched armor: roll the critical at the same time as the attack roll. Use the critical roll to determine area struck, then apply the AT for that location.

**Compatibility rule**: head and limb protection uses the same AT as torso if appropriate type. Light = AT 2–3, Medium = AT 4–6, Heavy = AT 7–10. Otherwise treat Light as AT 3, Medium as AT 5, Heavy as AT 9.
